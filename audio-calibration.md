# Audio Calibration Window (30-Second Automatic Adjustment)

In the current testing environment, the audio capture system uses an automatic calibration window of approximately 30 seconds at the beginning of each recording session. During this initial period, the system analyzes the surrounding acoustic environment in order to establish a baseline for noise levels and microphone sensitivity.

This short calibration phase allows the system to adjust parameters such as gain and background noise tolerance before continuous signal observation begins. The goal is to reduce the influence of environmental interference and improve the stability of subsequent audio measurements.

The 30-second window does not represent a fixed limitation of the system itself. Rather, it functions as a practical initialization stage commonly used in signal processing environments to stabilize the audio input before analysis.

After this calibration window, the system proceeds with normal audio monitoring, allowing environmental sounds and voice signals to be captured and analyzed within the defined observation framework.
