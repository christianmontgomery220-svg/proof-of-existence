Section 01

Project Overview

Anonymo AI is a real-time audio capture and frequency analysis system developed as part of the TCF/TFB research framework. The system is designed to capture, visualize, and annotate voice and physiological audio signals through a browser-based interface, using professional-grade microphones and external sensors.

The current phase focuses on voice audio analysis — capturing vocal frequency, amplitude, and spectral patterns in real time — with future integration planned for external physiological sensors (pulse/wrist-based).

"This system operates at the intersection of voice analysis, physiological monitoring, and structured research documentation — designed for precision, reproducibility, and multi-angle observation."
Section 02

Research Context & Authorization

All sessions conducted using this system are carried out under informed consent, in compliance with applicable research ethics standards. Participants are briefed on the nature of data collection before any session begins.

Data collected is used exclusively for research purposes within the Anonymo AI framework. No data is shared publicly without explicit authorization.

All research sessions require signed informed consent from participants. The researcher holds appropriate professional credentials to conduct this type of observational research.
Section 03

Sensor Architecture — The Audio Triangle

The core observation model is built around what the framework calls the "Audio Triangle" — a multi-sensor configuration that captures audio signals from different positions simultaneously, enabling cross-referenced analysis that eliminates distance variables and provides richer data.

Sensor Position Capture Type Primary Variable
Lapel / Mouth proximity Voice — direct capture Vocal frequency, articulation
Wrist / Pulse sensor Physiological — indirect Body resonance, physiological state
External / Ambient Environmental noise Background interference classification

An important methodological note: when the wrist sensor is the primary capture device and the phone/microphone is at a distance, the logical interpretation inverts — the distance variable shifts from the wrist to the voice source. The system accounts for this inversion across all observation variants.

Within this triangular observation model, the current framework maps multiple observation variants — each representing a distinct combination of sensor positions, distances, and signal priorities. The full variant matrix is documented separately within the TCF/TFB framework.

Section 05

Collaborative AI Framework

This research project employs a distributed AI collaboration model, where different AI systems are assigned to specific domains of the work:

Platform Domain
Genspark (Spark) Audio analysis system — interface development, frequency visualization
Manus Physiology — wrist sensor integration, body signal analysis
GPT (OpenAI) Research documentation, writing assistance, theoretical framework
Gemini (Google) Field studies, data cross-referencing, applied research

All AI contributions are assistive in nature. The conceptual framework, research design, structural decisions, and interpretation of results remain the exclusive intellectual work of the human author.

Section 06

Methodological Note

Hybrid Research Process:
This work was developed through a hybrid research process. Artificial intelligence tools were used as writing and drafting assistance in later stages of documentation, while the conceptual framework, structural design, and core ideas were developed by the human author.

The use of multiple AI platforms in this project reflects a deliberate methodological choice to leverage specialized capabilities across different research domains, while maintaining unified authorship and intellectual responsibility.
